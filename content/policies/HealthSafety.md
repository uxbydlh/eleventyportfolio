**For: Give and Grow CIC**    

**Effective Date: 01/09/2025**    

### **1\.  Purpose**

This policy outlines our commitment to maintaining a safe, healthy, and respectful remote working environment for all tutors, students, and staff. While we operate virtually, we recognize the importance of digital safety, mental wellbeing, and ergonomic practices.

### **2\. Scope**

This policy applies to:

* All employees and freelance tutors  
* Students and their guardians  
* Administrative and support staff

### **3\. Mental Health and Wellbeing**

We are committed to supporting mental health through:

* Encouraging regular breaks between sessions  
* Promoting a healthy work-life balance  
* Offering flexible scheduling to reduce burnout

### **4\.  Ergonomic and Physical Safety**

Tutors and staff are encouraged to:

* Set up a comfortable and ergonomic workstation  
* Use adjustable chairs, proper lighting, and screen positioning  
* Take regular breaks to stretch and move  
* Report any physical discomfort related to remote work

### **5\.  Digital Safety and Safeguarding**

We prioritize online safety by:

* Using secure platforms for tutoring sessions  
* Ensuring all tutors undergo safeguarding training  
* Prohibiting recording of sessions without consent  
* Regular Monitoring of interactions for professionalism and appropriateness  
* Reporting and investigating any concerns or breaches immediately

### **6\. Student Safety and Conduct**

Tutors must:

* Maintain professional boundaries at all times  
* Avoid private communication with students outside approved channels  
* Report any safeguarding concerns to the designated officer  
* Ensure sessions are appropriate, engaging and inclusive

### **7\. Incident Reporting**

All health, safety, or safeguarding concerns must be reported to:

* **Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL): Eva Mampilly- give.and.grow.cic@gmail.com**  
* Reports will be handled confidentially and in accordance with legal obligations

### **8\. Policy Review**

This policy will be reviewed annually or as needed to reflect changes in legislation, technology, or operational practices.
