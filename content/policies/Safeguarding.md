## **1\. Policy Statement**

Give and Grow CIC is committed to safeguarding and promoting the welfare of all children and young people involved in our services. We believe all children have the right to learn in a safe and secure environment, and we take our duty of care seriously in both online and in-person contexts.

---

## **2\. Scope of This Policy**

This policy applies to all staff, volunteers, tutors, and representatives of Give and Grow CIC. It covers all situations where they may come into contact with children and young people through our tutoring and outreach activities.

---

## **3\. Definitions**

* **Child:** Anyone under the age of 18\.

* **Safeguarding:** Protecting children from harm, abuse, exploitation, and ensuring their well-being.

* **Abuse:** Can be physical, emotional, sexual, or through neglect. Abuse can happen online as well as in person.

---

### **The DSL is responsible for:**

* Receiving and handling safeguarding concerns

* Deciding on appropriate action or referrals

## **5\. Code of Conduct for Tutors and Staff**

### **All tutors and staff must:**

* Treat all children with respect and dignity. Whatever the race, gender, or sexuality of the student, the tutor must ensure their pupil is not made to feel discriminated against.

* Keep communication professional and transparent, Employ appropriate language with their student

* Avoid private messaging with students (use parent/tutor group chats where possible) only contact through company email.

* Never record tutoring sessions without written parental consent

* All tutors must ensure that the environment does not display any inappropriate images or video during any online lesson.
* All tutors must arrive on time for their online lesson

* All tutors must ensure that if no parent/responsible adult is present during an online Lesson session, they should check that the student is comfortable to continue the session. If they are not comfortable, they can terminate the session.

---

## **6\. Online Safety Guidelines**

### **To ensure online safety:**

* All tutoring must take place on secure, pre-approved platforms (e.g. Zoom, Google Meet)

* Tutors must ensure a parent/guardian is aware of each session

* Tutors should be in a quiet, neutral background environment when teaching

* Sessions may be observed randomly for quality and safeguarding assurance

---

## **7\. Recognising and Responding to Concerns**

### **If a tutor or staff member suspects that a child is at risk of harm, they must:**

* Immediately report the concern to the DSL

* Write down what was seen, heard, or disclosed (factual notes only)

* Never promise confidentiality to a child; always explain you may need to pass on information for their safety

* In an emergency (e.g. a child is in immediate danger), contact emergency services (999) and inform the DSL.

---

## **8\. Confidentiality and Data Protection**

Safeguarding records are confidential and stored securely. Only the DSL and relevant authorities will have access. Personal data is handled in line with our \[Privacy Policy\] and in compliance with the UK GDPR.

Any information you acquire in your role as a tutor must be considered confidential \- this includes not just contact information (such as phone numbers, residential addresses, the school they attend), but also your pupil's work, and any comments or grades relating to it.

If your pupil is under the age of 18 you must ensure that a parent or guardian provides consent for the tuition to take place.

### **In addition to these obligations, you must ensure that you do not:**

* Search for and befriend your pupils on social media sites.
* Open up any form of communication, either face to face, over the phone or online, that does not pertain to your tuition.
* Make any suggestive, lewd, derogatory or discriminatory remarks in your pupil's presence.
* Attempt to obtain any details about their social media accounts, or extraneous details regarding their social life.
* Openly or surreptitiously take photographs or videos of your pupils, or request that they send you any photographs or videos of themselves.
* Coerce them to engage in any illegal activity.

---

## **9\. Allegations Against Staff or Volunteers**

**Any concerns about the conduct of a tutor or volunteer must be reported to the DSL immediately. If the concern involves the DSL, it should be reported to the chair or governing contact of the CIC.**

---

## **10\. Training and Awareness**

* All tutors and staff must receive a copy of this policy before working with students

* Basic safeguarding training will be offered or required

* Tutors must confirm in writing that they have read and understood this policy
